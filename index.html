Who Am I?

I'm a student, a programmer, and a maker.

What do I love to do?

I have been into STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Math) since a kid. Starting to learn programming at age of ten, I fell in love with computer science ever since. I have won the first prize in the National Informatics Olympiad (NOI) in China and then I took AP computer science and joined the computer science team in my high school during my sophomore year. I devoted four hours a week to training for our team for ACM contest. I am the group leader responsible for collecting group ideas and converting them into Java to execute it and solve the problem. Carrying forward this interest, I started learning robotics and attending in a robotics team since my elementary school. I have been a member of school robotics team in freshman and sophomore years in high school. I devoted two hours on each weekday. Every year our team attended FTC (First Tech Challenge) and I would cooperate with my teammates to provide a program for our robots. My third interest is math. I learned pre-calculus and then AP calculus BC in 9th and 10th grades. I attended math team for two years and devoted two hours a week to team activities. I enjoyed computer science the most not only because of the length of my involvement, but I am attracted by the beauty of all kinds of algorithms and data structures to process data, the art of solving a difficult problem by using precise languages such as Java and C.
